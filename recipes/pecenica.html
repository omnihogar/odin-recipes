Spit roasted piglet

Perfect for family gatherings, celebrations and holidays. A staple of south-eastern Europe, a pechenje is a welcome sight and always a cause for celebration.

It contains the following ingridients:

  • Piglet (about 23-33 Kg, depending on availability and need)
  • Salt
  • Oil (olive or sunflower)

Instructions:

  1. Make incisions on the slaughtered and cleaned piglet for salting (buns, neck - basically anywhere where the meat is too thick for salt to penetrate)
  2. Salt inside and in incisions, depending on type of roast spit, mount and tie up (legs to each other and to spit)
  3. Place next to the fires (one head height, one leg height)
  4. Keep well oiled while gradually connecting the fires, make sure it spins equally but not too fast
  5. When the skin cracks, it's nearing done, wait a little more for the thicker parts, though be carefull of the ribs
  6. Cut into pieces and serve on a platter, along with young onions, horse radish and/or russian salad
